Many homeowners only start thinking about refinancing when the lock-in period is almost over. By then, they may have less time to compare offers properly, give notice to the current bank, or spot hidden costs.

Here are seven common mistakes:

* Waiting until the last minute
* Looking only at headline interest rates
* Ignoring clawbacks or subsidy conditions
* Forgetting legal and valuation costs
* Not checking whether repricing is available
* Assuming your existing bank will offer the best retention package
* Failing to compare total cost, not just monthly instalment

The smartest borrowers usually start reviewing options months before the lock-in ends. That gives them room to compare banks, negotiate, and decide whether repricing or refinancing is the better route.

You do not need to predict every market move. You just need enough time to make a clean, informed decision instead of a rushed one.
