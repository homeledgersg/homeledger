If you are buying an HDB flat, one of the biggest loan decisions is whether to take an HDB concessionary loan or a bank loan.

At first glance, the answer may seem obvious. Bank loans can sometimes offer lower rates than the HDB concessionary rate. But the better loan depends on more than just the headline interest figure.

An HDB concessionary loan offers more predictability. It is tied to the CPF Ordinary Account rate plus 0.1 percent, which is why many buyers see it as a more stable option.

A bank loan may offer a lower rate, especially in a softer rate environment. That can reduce your monthly repayment and total interest cost. But bank loans may also come with lock-ins, repricing rules, and floating-rate exposure depending on the package.

Here is a simple way to compare them:

* HDB loan: more stable, more predictable, often easier for cautious buyers
* Bank loan: potentially cheaper, but may involve more rate risk and package complexity

A buyer who values certainty may prefer the HDB route even if it costs a little more. A buyer who wants to optimise monthly repayments may prefer a bank package.

The key is to choose based on your own comfort level, not just what looks cheapest today. A loan should fit your budget, your stress tolerance, and your long-term plans for the property.
