If you are buying property in Singapore, two rules can shape your home loan outcome more than you expect: TDSR and MSR.

These terms sound technical, but the idea is straightforward. They are affordability limits used to make sure borrowers do not take on more housing debt than they can reasonably manage.

TDSR stands for Total Debt Servicing Ratio. It looks at all your debt obligations compared with your gross monthly income.

MSR stands for Mortgage Servicing Ratio. It applies to certain housing purchases, especially HDB flats and some executive condominiums, and focuses specifically on your mortgage repayment.

The most useful way to remember the difference is:

* TDSR looks at total debt
* MSR looks at mortgage burden for qualifying housing purchases

This matters because some buyers assume that if they earn a decent income, they will automatically qualify for the loan size they want. But existing debts such as car loans, credit card balances, or personal loans can reduce borrowing capacity.

That is why loan approval sometimes comes in lower than expected, even when the property itself looks affordable on paper.

Understanding TDSR and MSR early helps you set a realistic budget, avoid disappointment, and focus only on homes that truly fit your financial position.
