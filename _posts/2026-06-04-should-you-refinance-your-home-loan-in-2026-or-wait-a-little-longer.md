Many Singapore homeowners are asking the same question in 2026: should I refinance my home loan now, or wait to see if rates fall a little more?

It is a fair question. Mortgage rates have eased from their earlier highs, and borrowers are finally seeing more attractive packages again. But waiting for the “perfect” rate can sometimes cost more than acting on a clearly better one that is already available.

A simple way to think about this is to compare your current loan against what is available today.

If your current package is still much more expensive than the market, refinancing may already make sense. Even a difference of 0.5 percent can change your monthly instalment and your total interest cost over the next few years.

Before making a move, check these points carefully:

* Is your lock-in period ending soon?
* Is there a subsidy clawback or penalty?
* Do you need to give advance notice to your current bank?
* Are there legal or valuation fees involved?
* Does the new package still make sense after those costs?

Many borrowers focus only on the headline interest rate. That is a mistake. A slightly higher package with better flexibility, lower fees, or fewer restrictions can sometimes be the better choice.

If your lock-in is ending within the next few months, this is usually the right time to start reviewing your options. The goal is not to predict the market perfectly. The goal is to avoid overpaying when a better package is already within reach.
