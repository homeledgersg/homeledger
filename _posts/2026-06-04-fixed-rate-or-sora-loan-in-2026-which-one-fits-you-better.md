In 2026, Singapore borrowers are again deciding between fixed-rate home loans and SORA-based floating packages.

Both can be good choices. The real question is not which one is “best” in general. The better question is: which one fits your financial situation better?

A fixed-rate package gives you certainty for a set period, usually two or three years. That means your repayments stay predictable during the lock-in period.

A SORA package usually starts lower, but it can move over time. If interest rates rise, your monthly repayment may rise too. If rates stay soft or fall, you may benefit from lower interest costs.

A fixed rate may suit you if:

* You prefer stable monthly repayments
* Your budget is tight and you want certainty
* You dislike monitoring rate movements

A SORA package may suit you if:

* You are comfortable with some rate fluctuation
* You want a lower starting rate
* You can handle repayment changes if the market moves

Many borrowers make the mistake of choosing purely based on the lowest advertised number. But peace of mind also has value. If a fixed package helps you budget with confidence, paying a little more may still be worth it.

On the other hand, if your income is stable and you have more room in your monthly budget, a floating package may offer better value over time. The right answer depends on your own risk profile, not just the market headline.
